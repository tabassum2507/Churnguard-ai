---
title: "Reading Your First FlowMetric Report"
category: onboarding
---

Your first FlowMetric report will show a lot of information at once, so it helps to know where to focus. The top row — sometimes called the KPI strip — shows your most important numbers at a glance: active projects, tasks completed this week, percentage of budget used, and overall team utilization rate.

Below that, the timeline view shows how your milestones are tracking against their planned dates. Green means on schedule, amber means at risk of being late, and red means the date has passed without completion. For your first week, the most useful habit is checking the amber milestones every morning — these still have time to recover if someone takes action now.

The activity feed on the right shows what happened across your projects in the last twenty-four hours. It's a quick way to stay informed without asking each teammate for a status update.

Click into any milestone to see what's blocking it and who's responsible. The more consistently your team logs time and updates task statuses, the more accurate these reports become. Even after one week of active use, the patterns start to get meaningful.
