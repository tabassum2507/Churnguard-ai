---
title: "When to Upgrade or Downgrade Your FlowMetric Plan"
category: plan_comparison
---

The right time to upgrade is usually when you find yourself working around FlowMetric rather than inside it. On Free, that moment arrives when you hit the three-user or one-project limit and have to archive things that are still active. Upgrading to Starter makes sense the moment a second project becomes important to your business.

The move from Starter to Pro is typically driven by one of three things: your team grows past ten people, you need API access to connect FlowMetric to another system, or you want automated alerts and scheduled reports so you're not checking the dashboard manually every day.

Enterprise conversations usually start when a company needs SSO for compliance reasons, has data residency requirements, or wants a named account manager for onboarding and ongoing support.

Downgrading is always possible at the end of your current billing cycle — your data is preserved for ninety days after a downgrade in case you need to return to a higher tier. You won't lose any historical data during the transition, though some features will become read-only until you upgrade again.
