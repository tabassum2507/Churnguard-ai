---
title: "Managing Notification Preferences in FlowMetric"
category: config_fixes
---

FlowMetric sends notifications by email by default, but you have a lot of control over exactly what arrives and where. Go to Settings, then Notifications, to see the full list of notification types organized by category.

You can turn off categories you don't care about. For example, if you don't want an email every time someone comments on a task you're not directly involved in, you can switch comment notifications to mentions-only, so you only hear about it when someone specifically tags you.

Digest mode is useful for people who find individual notifications distracting. It batches everything from the day into a single email delivered at a time you choose. If you've connected Slack, you can route some notifications to Slack and leave others as email — many teams prefer to get milestone alerts in Slack and billing notifications by email.

Mobile push notifications are configured separately in the mobile app settings and won't be affected by changes you make in the web interface. Quiet hours are available as a workspace-level setting, suppressing all non-critical notifications during the hours you define, including weekends if you want a true work-life boundary. Workspace admins set quiet hours for everyone, but individual users can adjust their personal window.
